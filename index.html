Week 3

28 July 2023


In week 3, we looked at the history of web design from 1998 to 2008, highlighting pivotal moments such as the introduction of partially responsive websites in 2001. I thought this was particularly interesting to think about as I find it frustrating when websites don’t function the same across different platforms, so I can only imagine how frustrating it was before responsive websites.

We also explored significant developments like the launch of Google Analytics in 2005, which plays a crucial role in modern digital marketing. I am particularly interested in learning more about Google Analytics as my second major is marketing and it would be helpful for the future.

I discovered that the little icon that appears in the tab of a website is called a favicon. The first version of a favicon was released in 1999. I learnt that they also enhance user experience and re-enhance brand recognition which makes a lot of sense to me. I find them particularly useful when you open a lot of tabs as it is easy to navigate to the website you want by looking at the favicons on each tab.

I learnt about the emergence of skeuomorphic design that was mainstreamed by Apple with the release of the iPhone in 2007. I personally think skeuomorphic design looks a bit tacky, but I understand how helpful it would have been as people transitioned to using digital versions of well-known tools such as the calculator, calendar, and keypad. Resembling their real-world counterparts helped users understand how to use the new interfaces as they look like a precedent they already used.

I found the importance of setting clear design goals using the SMART framework and the significance of understanding users' goals by adopting their perspective very interesting. The venn diagram workshop helped us illustrate designer and user goals, emphasizing the importance of meeting users' needs when designing websites. For Assignment 1 I assured I went into a lot more depth than the workshop exercise, making the user and designer goals more specific. The SMART goals framework, and both my groups venn diagram and my individual venn diagram for Assignment 1 can be seen above.

Colour theory taught me to use RGB or hexadecimal colours for web design, and I found that out of all the colour schemes complementary was my favourite so I will use it for my skincare website.

In terms of typography I learnt about rem and em type sizing being the best for web design, which I’d never heard of before. I also found the contrast in paragraph width between web design and communication design intriguing. In web design, paragraphs shouldn't stretch across the entire page, as that would make the reader's gaze linger for too long and the reader will get bored, but if the paragraphs are excessively short, it forces the reader's eyes to continually dart. Therefore, I've gained a valuable insight into the importance of maintaining a comfortable paragraph width. The image above shoes how the second paragraph is better for web design.

In the coding lecture we explored GitHub, a cloud-based code repository. highlighting its commit and version control features, as well as repository setup and publishing. I think GitHub’s version control capabilities is a game changer. It will allow me to track the modifications I make in my code so I can use this to retrace my steps if I make a mistake when coding, which is bound to happen as I’m just starting my coding journey.

Week 7

8 September 2023


This week, we had no pre-recorded lecture due to the upcoming presentations for Assignment 2.1. The focus of the lecture was on composition, particularly exploring various types of grids. The hierarchical grid was my personal favourite. We also learnt that hierarchy in design can also be established through colour and contrast, as it grabs the users' attention. We also looked at the significance of scale, teaching us that larger items often signify greater importance. My takeaway from this is that in order to guide a user’s attention through your page it is important to make the things you want them to see first bigger, brighter, and bolder. I’ll be sure to use my concept of the three b’s in my future designs.

We then learnt about the concepts of affordance and mental models. Mental models are personalized constructs shaped by user experiences. Similarly, affordances are cues that guide user interactions on digital interfaces. An example of a mental model can be seen in the button in the image above, which many people associate with a dropdown feature that reveals a menu. This understanding is shaped by prior interactions with interfaces that employ a similar button design.

Reflecting on this, it's clear that mental models are rooted in personal beliefs rather than concrete facts. So, it's essential for designers to acknowledge that their perception of a system may not align with that of the users, highlighting the necessity of gaining an understanding of users to develop designs that truly cater to their needs.

Week 9

22 September 2023


This week we learnt more about responsive design which is a web design approach that aims to create websites that work seamlessly on a variety of devices. There are various responsive design techniques, such as fluid grids, flexible images, media queries, as well as the use of rem or em font measures. An interesting trick we learnt to see how other websites use responsive design was on Google Chrome. You can inspect the elements of a website by going view > developer > inspect elements. Each element is colour coded so you can figure out what each aspect the code relates to. I’ll definitely be using this trick to get inspiration on how to code certain features that I like on websites in the future.

We also learnt about the significance of alt text, which extends beyond simply describing images for users when they don't load fully. Accurate alt text helps promote inclusivity, especially for individuals with visual impairments who rely on screen readers to visualise the images on webpages. I had not previously considered the importance of alt text so I decided to research this further. I found that alt text also plays a crucial part in SEO, as it provides contextual information about the content to search engines, assisting them to correctly rank images in image searches (Moz, n.d.). Because of this I have made sure to add quality alt text for each of the images in this blog as well as my skincare website.

Week 10

29 September 2023


When considering Assignment 2.2, I initially deliberated between using a content management system (CMS) site or building my website from scratch. After thoroughly researching both options, I decided to opt for a CMS due to its user-friendly features. This choice allows me to create a fully functional website without compromising on the design elements I had planned in Assignment 1. Some of the elements in my wireframe were beyond my coding abilities in HTML and CSS, so I'm happy I decided to use a CMS for this assignment.

I have chosen Webflow to create my website. Even though I had never used a CMS site before, the in-site tutorials provided by Webflow and the educational resources on Webflow University's YouTube channel have helped me tremendously with my web design. Furthermore, I studied various Webflow templates to gain insights into how each element functions and how they have been used in different contexts. This has helped me understand the intricacies of Webflows elements.

I've come across several challenges in Webflow, like when my text and images didn't line up properly and when my typefaces weren’t installed in Webflow. I eventually realised that placing the text and images in the same quick stack fixed this issue and I also learnt how to upload custom fonts to Webflow. I have learned from these frustrating moments that attention to detail and problem-solving are crucial when designing a website, and this skill will be valuable in all my future design projects.

Overall, I am finding Assignment 2.2 to be both tough and exciting because I'm challenging myself to learn new skills, so that I can curate a website as close to my wireframe from Assignment 1 as possible.
